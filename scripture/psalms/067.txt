Let God arise, and let his enemies be scattered; let them that hate him flee before his face.
As smoke vanisheth, so let them vanish; as wax melteth from before fire, so let the wicked perish from before God.
And let the righteous be glad; let them rejoice before God; let them delight with gladness.
Sing unto God, chant unto his name; make way for him that rideth upon the setting of the sun, the Lord is his name, and rejoice before him.
They shall be troubled from before him, the father of the fatherless, and judge of the widows; God is in his holy habitation.
God settleth a solitary in a house; he bringeth out them in chains with strength; likewise them that provoke, that dwell in tombs.
O God, when thou wentest forth before thy people, when thou didst pass through the wilderness,
Earth did quake, for the heavens dropped from before the God of Sinai, from before the God of Israel.
Thou, O God, wilt freely ordain rain for thine inheritance; yea, it became weak, but thou thyself restored it.
Thy living creatures dwell therein; thou hast prepared for the poor in thy goodness, O God.
The Lord shall give speech with great power unto them that bring good tidings;
The king of the powers of the beloved, to divide spoils for the beauty of the house.
If ye should sleep among the lots, there shall be wings of a dove covered with silver, and its back with greenness of gold.
When the heavenly one setteth apart kings over her, they shall be white as snow in Selmon.
A mountain of God is a fat mountain; a curdled mountain, a fat mountain.
Wherefore do ye suppose, ye curdled mountains, the mountain in which God was well pleased to dwell? Yea, for the Lord shall dwell therein unto an end.
The chariots of God are ten thousandfold, yea, thousands of them abounding; the Lord is among them in Sinai, in his holy place.
Thou hast ascended on high, thou hast led captivity captive; thou hast received gifts in man, yea, for those in rebellion, that thou mightest dwell therein.
Blessed be the Lord God, blessed be the Lord day by day; the God of our salvation shall guide us rightly.
Our God is the God who saves, and unto the Lord, the Lord are the pathways out from death.
But God shall crush the heads of his enemies, the hairy crown of them that walk in their trespasses.
The Lord said, I will return out from Bashan, I will return by depths of sea,
That thy foot may be dipped in blood of thine enemies, and the tongue of thy dogs in the same.
Thy goings have been seen, O God, the goings of my God, the king, in the holy place.
Rulers went before, joined next by them singing, in midst of maidens playing timbrels.
Bless ye God in the churches, the Lord from the fountains of Israel.
There is Benjamin, the younger, in rapture; the princes of Judah their leaders, the princes of Zebulun, the princes of Naphtali.
Command, O God, in thy strength; O God, strengthen that which thou hast wrought in us.
Because of thy temple at Jerusalem, kings shall bring gifts unto thee.
Rebuke the wild beasts of the reed, the gathering of the bulls among the heifers of the people, that those tried by silver be not shut out; scatter thou the nations that desire war.
Ambassadors shall come out from Egypt; Ethiopia shall hasten to stretch out her hand unto God.
Sing unto God, ye kingdoms of the earth; sing praises unto the Lord.
To him that rideth upon the heaven of the heaven in the east; behold, he will give his voice a voice of power.
Give glory unto God; his magnificence is over Israel, and his power is in the clouds.
Wonderful is God in his saints; the God of Israel is he that will give power and strength unto his people. Blessed be God.
