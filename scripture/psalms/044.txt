My heart hath poured forth a good word; I speak of my works unto the king; my tongue is the pen of a scribe that writeth swiftly.
Flourishing in beauty beyond the sons of men, grace was poured out upon thy lips; therefore God hath blessed thee unto the age.
Gird Thy sword upon thy thigh, O mighty one, in thy flourish and in thy beauty,
And bend thy bow, and prosper, and be king, because of truth and meekness and righteousness, and thy right hand shall guide thee wondrously.
Thine arrows are sharp, O mighty one, sharp in the heart of the enemies of the king; the peoples shall fall under thee.
Thy throne, O God, is unto the age of age; a sceptre of righteousness is the sceptre of thy kingdom.
Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity; therefore God, even thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.
Myrrh and aloes and cassia perfume thy garments, from the ivory palaces whereby they have made thee glad.
The daughters of kings are in thy honour; the queen stood at thy right hand, clothed in golden raiment, arrayed in many colours.
Hear, O daughter, and see, and incline thine ear; forget thine own people, and the house of thy father.
For the king shall desire thy beauty, for he is thy Lord, and thou shalt worship him.
And the daughters of Tyre shall bow down to him with gifts; the rich among the people shall entreat thy face.
